Multiple linear regression is performed to create a model for warbler mass in relation to four variable: wing length (in millimeters), sex (male or female), culmen length (in millimeters), and tarsus length (in millimeters).

-The model was assessed for fit in multiple ways, with ggpairs() indicating a normal distribution of all variables.

-AIC was used to determine the proper balance of model fit and variable, with the above mentioned multiple linear regression model yielding the lowest Akaike Information Criterion value (AIC = 1191.78). AIC is a relative measure and is useful in comparing models but needs to b used in conjunction with other assessments.

-When using pairs() to assess the assumptions of the model, all assumptions were met (residuals vs.fitted values/homoscedasticity, normal distribution of the residuals, and cooks distance of residuals) although there is one warbler data point that is an outlier, a male warbler with a mass of 14.5 grams. Should also be conscious about interaction between predictor variables. Variance Inflation Factor (VIF) values show this to be less likely

Discussion of Final Model

Mass = 3.596 + 0.071(wing_r) -1.034(Male) + 0.142(bill_culmen) + 0.065(tarsus1)

  • wing_r: The coefficient of 0.071 for wing length (millimeters) indicates that on average, we expect a 0.071 gram increase in warbler mass for each additional millimeter in right wing length.

  • sex male: The coefficient of -1.034 for males indicates that on average, if all other variables are equal, a male warblers mass to be 1.034 less than a female warbler. Female is the reference sex.

  • bill_culmen: The coefficient of 0.142 for for bill culmen length (millimeters) indicates that on average, we expect a 0.142 gram increase in warbler mass for each additional millimeter of bill culmen length. The culmen goes from the tip of the bill to the feathers at top of head.

  • tarsus1 Value:The coefficient of 0.065 for tarsus length (millimeters) indicates that on average, we expect a 0.065 gram increase in warbler mass for each additional millimeter of tarsus (foot) length.

  • Intercept Value: The coefficient value (3.596) means that a female warbler with zero values for all the continuous explanatory variables would have a mass of 3.596 grams, which is not a very useful statement.
